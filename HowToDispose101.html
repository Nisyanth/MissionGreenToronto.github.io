How to Dispose 101

An article by Connie Zhang

Have you ever wondered what to do with an old laptop, or a broken iPhone? Disposing electronics is actually very simple!
Technology has taken over the world, and most of North America possesses a form of it! Cell phones, laptops, batteries, PS4s, digital clocks, telephones, and iPads are only a small portion of the technology used. But do the people of Toronto really know how to dispose of electronics?
An easy way you could get rid of your old electronics is to leave it outside your building, beside your regular garbage! Ensure large items (eg. computers) are placed on the ground, and smaller items (eg. cell phones) are placed in a clear plastic bag or open cardboard box.
You must remember that batteries are hazardous, to a certain degree. Batteries contain different toxins that may be harmful, which is why they must be disposed with caution. Hazardous waste cannot be organized in your garbage or recycling bin! The easiest thing to do is to arrange a free pick-up from the City of Toronto!
Simply dial 311, and you can keep others and the environment safe!
Disposing correctly is not hard, and it should not feel like a chore. In order to keep our Earth sustainable, we must be aware of the harm we cause it!
Keep us all safe and dispose responsibly!
