KILLERS IN THE
Park

The orcas' fearsome reputation classes them among other terrifying predators like lions and crocodiles. Yet, while the aforementioned animals kill approximately 100 and 1,000 humans each year, respectively, there have been no reported human deaths due to a wild orca. Such a fact is certainly difficult to believe. Although the reason for why killer whales do not hunt humans is still unclear, studies in the past conducted by marine biologists have shown that killer whales are, in fact, playful and friendly creatures.
